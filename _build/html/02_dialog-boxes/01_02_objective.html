State Variable vs. Objective#

One of the first choices you will make when designing your study, is what it is that you plan to measure (or your “state variable”; a formal measure that summarizes the state of a community or population at a particular time (Wearn & Glover Kapfer, 2017))

Since this concept might be easy to confuse with Survey Objective(s)](./glossary_ref/09_glossary.md#survey_objectives) ) (and since “objective” is often referred to interchangeably with “state variable”), consider the following figure:

Objective vs. State Variable

The following sections include definitions for each option, with a few additional notes:

  • Species inventory: Rapid assessment surveys to determine what species are present in a given area at a given point in time; there is no attempt made to quantify aspects of communities or populations (Wearn & Glover-Kapfer, 2017).

  • Species diversity & richness

    • Species richness: The number of species found in the community/area measured

    • Species diversity: Species diversity is more complex, and includes a measure of the number of species in a community, and a measure of the abundance of each species. Species diversity is usually described by an index, such as Shannon’s Index H’.” Pyron, 2010

    • Note that there are multiple “levels” to Species diversity & richness, these include:

      • Alpha richness (&alpha): The number of species at the level of an individual camera location Wearn & Glover-Kapfer, 2017

      • Beta-diversity (&beta): The differences between the communities or, more formally, the variance among the communities Wearn & Glover-Kapfer, 2017

      • Gamma richness (&gamma): The number of species across a whole study area Wearn & Glover-Kapfer, 2017

  • Occupancy: The probability a site is occupied by the species MacKenzie et al., 2002. Occupancy is also highly suitable for evaluating broad-scale patterns of species distribution Wearn & Glover-Kapfer, 2017.

  • Abundance (Relative vs. Absolute)

    • Absolute abundance / Population size: The number of individuals in a population Wearn & Glover-Kapfer, 2017.
